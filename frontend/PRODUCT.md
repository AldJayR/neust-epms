# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

web

## Users

The primary users are NEUST faculty members preparing extension project proposals, tracking implementation, and submitting required reports. Their work is often procedural and deadline-driven, so the interface must make current status and required actions easy to understand.

Secondary users are the reviewing and administrative roles that move the same work forward: RET Chairs, the Extension Director, and system administrators.

## Product Purpose

The NEUST Extension Project Monitoring System gives faculty and reviewing offices one place to move extension work from proposal through implementation and reporting. Success means users complete each required step accurately and on time.

## Positioning

EPMS is the single dependable workflow from proposal through implementation and reporting.

## Brand Personality

Dependable, clear, and calm. The experience should earn institutional confidence while staying focused and easy to navigate. Linear is the interaction-quality reference for fast, consistent workflows with unmistakable status and ownership.

## Anti-references

EPMS should not resemble a generic SaaS dashboard. Avoid decorative metrics, excessive card grids, gradients, and startup-style visual flourishes that compete with operational work.

## Design Principles

- Keep the next required action unmistakable.
- Preserve one coherent workflow across faculty, review, and administrative roles.
- Make status and ownership trustworthy at a glance.
- Prefer calm operational clarity over decoration.
- Help users finish required work accurately and on time.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA. Future interface work must support keyboard access, visible focus, sufficient contrast, reduced motion, and robust accessible labels.
