Now we convert the **problem space into an opportunity space**.

A good HMW statement should be broad enough to allow multiple solutions, but narrow enough to address a real user need. We should avoid embedding features into the question.

Weak:

> How might we create a dashboard that shows project status?

This already assumes the solution is a dashboard.

Stronger:

> How might we help project leaders understand where their project is and what happens next?

The HMW statements below come directly from the problem statements, journey maps, pain points, accessibility and equity analysis, and the workflow represented in the current use cases. 

# Core HMW Statements

These should guide the overall product concept.

## HMW-01: Project Journey Clarity

> **How might we help Faculty Project Leaders understand where their project currently is, what has already happened, and what will happen next?**

This opens possibilities such as:

* project timelines;
* lifecycle views;
* contextual status explanations;
* project history;
* stage summaries.

But none of those are prescribed yet.

---

## HMW-02: Action and Responsibility

> **How might we help users immediately understand whether they need to act, wait, or follow up—and who currently owns the next action?**

This addresses one of the deepest problems in the system.

Every workflow state should eventually answer:

**Do I need to do something?**

If yes:

**What?**

If no:

**Who are we waiting for?**

---

## HMW-03: Work Prioritization

> **How might we help RET Chairs and Director/Admin users identify what requires their attention first without requiring them to search through multiple modules?**

This opens the design space for:

* work queues;
* priority views;
* alerts;
* attention states;
* task summaries.

It does not prematurely assume a dashboard.

---

## HMW-04: Preventive Compliance

> **How might we help users anticipate and resolve upcoming deadlines, expirations, and incomplete requirements before they become administrative problems?**

This reframes the system from:

> detect failure

to:

> help prevent failure.

---

## HMW-05: Equitable Participation

> **How might we enable users with different levels of experience, ability, connectivity, available time, and institutional knowledge to complete essential workflows with comparable independence and confidence?**

This is the broadest HMW statement, but it should act as a **design lens across every concept**, not as one isolated feature.

# The HMW statements we should actually carry into Ideate

We should not ideate against all 60 questions independently. That would fragment the product and produce feature sprawl.

I would consolidate them into **eight design challenges**:

### Design Challenge 1 — The Continuous Project Journey

> **How might we help users experience proposal preparation, review, approval, activation, implementation, reporting, and closure as one understandable project journey?**

### Design Challenge 2 — The Right Action at the Right Time

> **How might we help every user immediately understand what requires their action, what they are waiting for, and who currently owns the next step?**

### Design Challenge 3 — Confident Preparation and Recovery

> **How might we help users prepare complete submissions, understand problems, recover from errors, and revise successfully without unnecessary repetition?**

### Design Challenge 4 — Attention Instead of Information Overload

> **How might we help RET Chairs and Director/Admin users identify what matters now without searching through large numbers of projects and modules?**

### Design Challenge 5 — Approval to Implementation

> **How might we make the transition from approved proposal to implementation-ready project understandable, coordinated, and safe?**

### Design Challenge 6 — Prevention Before Failure

> **How might we help users anticipate deadlines, expirations, delays, and incomplete requirements before they become urgent problems?**

### Design Challenge 7 — Multiple Responsibilities, One Coherent Experience

> **How might we support users who hold multiple responsibilities while keeping their personal work, oversight duties, visibility, and authority clear?**

### Design Challenge 8 — Equitable and Accessible Participation

> **How might we enable users with different abilities, devices, connectivity, experience, available time, and institutional knowledge to complete the same essential journeys with comparable independence and confidence?**

These eight should become the **actual input to the Ideate phase**.

The strongest move next is not to start designing screens. We should perform **idea generation per design challenge**—using methods like Crazy 8s, “worst possible idea,” analogous inspiration, and then concept clustering—before deciding whether the solution should be a project workspace, action center, readiness system, lifecycle timeline, or something we have not yet considered.
